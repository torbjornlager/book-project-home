Torbjörn Lager

Torbjörn Lager is a professor emeritus of general and computational linguistics. His PhD is in linguistics, but he has spent most of his research and development in the field of computational linguistics, with a broad range of interests spanning tools for corpus linguistics, tools for machine learning, and platforms for the development of multimodal dialogue systems.

In recent years he has nurtured a keen interest in Web Science, looking at the Web not only as a technical artefact but also as a social phenomenon. On the technical side, he has done web-related programming for fifteen years, mostly building research prototypes, and has given courses in web technology — in particular in his area of expertise, the Spoken Web (VoiceXML, SRGS, SSML). He is an invited expert in the W3C Voice Browser Working Group and is involved in the specification of State Chart XML (SCXML).

He is also interested in the Semantic Web — a natural fit given his background in logic and logic programming. On the non-technical side of Web Science, he believes linguistics has an interesting perspective to offer. With a background in philosophy, he is drawn to the Philosophy of the Web and to the interactions of technology, science and the humanities in general.
